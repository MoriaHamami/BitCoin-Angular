Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency that operates on a peer-to-peer network without the need for a central authority. It was created in 2009 by an anonymous individual or group of individuals using the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto. Bitcoin transactions are verified by network nodes through cryptography and recorded on a public ledger called a blockchain. The limited supply of Bitcoin, with a maximum of 21 million coins that can be mined, creates scarcity and is often cited as a key factor in its value. Despite its initial skepticism, Bitcoin has gained increasing adoption as a payment method and investment vehicle, with many businesses accepting it as payment and institutional investors investing in it as a hedge against inflation and a store of value.
